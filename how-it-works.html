The model

Why this costs you nothing

The zero-cost structure isn't a promotional offer — it's how Enertech's business model works. We earn revenue when residents charge their vehicles. That revenue funds the hardware, installation, and operations. Your property earns a share of it.

What Enertech covers

Everything — without exception

  • Commercial-grade Autel Level 2 and DC fast chargers
  • Full electrical scope — conduit, wiring, panel work, and upgrades
  • All municipal permits and utility interconnection
  • 24/7 remote monitoring and proactive maintenance
  • Resident billing and driver support — not your staff
  • Xcel rebate and Charge Ahead Colorado grant applications
  • Hardware replacement at end of life

What your property does

Sign the agreement and collect revenue

Your property provides the parking spaces and grants access for installation. That's the extent of your operational involvement.

  • Designate EV charging parking spaces
  • Allow Enertech crew site access during installation (typically 1–3 days)
  • Distribute the resident onboarding packet we provide
  • Receive monthly revenue share payments

Your staff never handle maintenance requests, billing disputes, or outage reports. Residents contact Enertech directly.

Free property assessment
20–30 minutes · No obligation

We start with a brief conversation about your property — number of units, parking layout, and how many residents you think already have or plan to get an EV. From there, we do a site review to understand the electrical infrastructure and determine the best placement and quantity of chargers.

We'll be honest if the economics don't make sense for your property. That's rare, but we'd rather tell you upfront than sign an agreement that doesn't work well for either side.

Installation
1–3 days on-site · Minimal disruption

Once permits are cleared, our installation crew comes on-site. For most multifamily properties, installation takes 1–3 days. We coordinate access with your property manager and work around parking availability to minimize disruption to residents.

We handle the full electrical scope — conduit runs, panel work, make-ready infrastructure for future expansion, and charger installation. Our crew cleans up completely before leaving.

Ongoing operations

What Enertech handles after go-live

The partnership doesn't end at installation. Enertech operates your charging program indefinitely — with no cost or operational lift to your property.

24/7 remote monitoring

Every charger on every Enertech site is monitored continuously via the Monta network. We identify issues proactively — before residents report them.

  • Real-time charger status and session data
  • Automated fault detection and alerts
  • Firmware and network software updates

On-site repair response

When a charger needs hands-on attention, we dispatch a local Colorado technician. We own the hardware — repairs are our responsibility, not yours.

  • Colorado-based technicians — not remote dispatch
  • Hardware replacement at end of life at no cost
  • Property staff are never involved in repair calls

Resident billing & driver support

Residents are billed directly through Monta — your office isn't in the billing loop. Our 24/7 driver support line handles all charging questions, access issues, and outage reports.

  • Per-session billing via Monta app or web — no RFID cards
  • 24/7 driver support: +1 888 895 7158
  • Billing disputes resolved by Enertech, not your staff

Revenue share

Your property earns passive income from your parking lot

Every charging session generates revenue. Enertech earns the portion that funds hardware, operations, and capital. Your property earns a meaningful share — paid monthly, with no administrative work on your end.

Revenue share percentages are negotiated per property and stated clearly in the partnership agreement. We don't publish a single number because every property is different — charger count, usage volume, and site economics all factor in. Your proposal will show exactly what to expect.

Colorado incentives

We navigate Colorado's EV incentive programs on your behalf

Xcel Energy rebates, Charge Ahead Colorado grants, and federal incentives can significantly reduce installation costs. Enertech handles every application — you never deal with the paperwork.

Xcel Energy territory
Xcel Energy

Multifamily EV rebate program

Xcel Energy offers make-ready infrastructure rebates and per-port equipment rebates for networked Level 2 chargers at multifamily properties in their service territory. Pre-project approval is required — Enertech applies before installation begins.

Current program details at xcelenergy.com
Income-qualified properties
State of Colorado

Charge Ahead Colorado grant

A state-funded grant program targeting EV charging at income-qualified and underserved multifamily properties — typically those serving households at or below 80% of Area Median Income. Can stack on top of Xcel rebates for qualifying properties.

Eligibility details at chargeaheadcolorado.org
Federal
IRS Section 30C

Alternative Fuel Vehicle Refueling Credit

In Enertech's zero-cost model, we own the equipment — so this credit accrues to Enertech, which is part of how we fund the program. Property owners considering self-funded installations should consult their tax advisor about 30C eligibility.
